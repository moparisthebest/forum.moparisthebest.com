Gotta Catch Them All:

You’d need a big group of VERY experienced programmers who are willing to work together and put alot of their own time into coding this game. It could be done but I doubt we have what it takes.

It’s actually not that difficult to write a MORPG. The three requirements that make the task much easier if you are not a multi-million dollar company are:

  • Drop the first “M”. Don’t aim for a “Massive” world right off the bat, or a “Massive” playerbase. Runescape is probably the most successful example of a game that started small and grew big. They only had 3 developers to begin with, remember. (It might have even been 2, with Tedder joining later, I can’t remember.) Aiming for a smaller game makes certain technical demands such as “how the hell do I maintain 1 million concurrent users efficiently” much easier, you can just write a fairly scalable system and worry about actually scaling it later on.

  • Don’t reinvent the wheel. Use a pre-existing rendering engine (such as OGRE), use a pre-existing network solution (such as Raknet).

  • Design, design, design. Don’t even think about starting to implement ANYTHING until you have at least a solid game design document and technical specification.

How do I know this? Because the people saying “an indie team can’t finish a MMORPG” are the same people who a few years ago were saying “an indie team can’t finish an FPS” - and I’ve worked on those.
