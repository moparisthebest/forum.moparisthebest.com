BobDole81 July 30, 2016, 4:30pm #11

I know this is old, but you didn‚Äôt really answer the question fully xxlegitxx. Certainly there are an infinite number of point solutions, but there‚Äôs only one linear solution.

First, the first equation given is weird‚Ä¶it could either refer to x - 2y + z = 8 or x - 2y + z + 8 = 0 => x - 2y + z = -8, but my guess is that it is the former. This does not affect the first step, which consists of finding the normal vectors of the planes:

x - 2y + z = 8
N = <1, -2, 1>

2x + y - z = 0
N = <2, 1, -1>

3x - 6y + 3z = 24
x - 2y + z = 8
N = <1, -2, 1>

Obviously, the first and third equations are equal (or at least parallel), and thus you are solving for not the point but line of intersection of equations 1 or 3 and 2.

You can find this line of intersection by taking the cross product of the normal vectors for equation 1 and equation 2 and then finding a point on the line of intersection (which boblinian did). Then use the vector/parametric form of a line to put the info together.

This may or may not have been what was required, but this a better way to do it imo
