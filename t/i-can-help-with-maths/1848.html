BobDole81 July 29, 2016, 8:00pm #19

Assuming it is suppose to be:

((cos(t) * cot(t)) - (sin(t) * tan(t)) * sin(t) * cos(t)
----------------------------------------------------------
                    (cos(t) - sin(t))

For the first part of the first line:
((cos(t) * cot(t)) = (cos(t) * cos(t)/sin(t)) = (cos^2(t) / sin(t))
since cot(t) = cos(t)/sin(t)

For the second part of the first line:
(sin(t) * tan(t)) = (sin(t) * sin(t)/cos(t)) = (sin^2(t) / cos(t))
since tan(t) = sin(t)/cos(t)

Now we have:

((cos^2(t) / sin(t)) - (sin^2(t) / cos(t))) * sin(t) * cos(t)
----------------------------------------------------------
                    (cos(t) - sin(t))

Since there is a sin(t) and cos(t) outside of this block, they can be multiplied in individually to result in:

(cos^3(t) - (sin^3(t))
----------------------
  (cos(t) - sin(t))

Looking at cos^3(t) - (sin^3(t) as a difference of two cubes, we get:

(cos(t) - sin(t))(cos^2(t) + cos(t)sin(t) + sin^2(t))
-----------------------------------------------------
                  (cos(t) - sin(t))

After which the (cos(t) - sin(t))'s cancel out. Next, we can use a pythagorean identity to work it down:

cos^2(t) + cos(t)sin(t) + sin^2(t)
(cos^2(t) + sin^2(t)) + cos(t)sin(t)
1 + cos(t)sin(t)

And 1 + cos(t)sin(t) is as far as I can get right now I can't think of whether I can simplify it any further right now, but I think it's right...went through it once quickly.
