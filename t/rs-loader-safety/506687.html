Best bet is to look through their applet viewer and see how they do it.

But what we’ve generally agreed upon is
-Spoofing the user agent. (Does their client even do this?)
-Grabbing the parameters from the webpage. They’re needed by the client, and needed by us now to decrypt the inner.pack.gz

and some ideas I’ve seen tossed around are
-Replicate the strict security permissions as if running from the webpage
-Hide/prevent access to our classes
-Provide stubs for some special browser libs, like the netscape.javascript package

Generally, try to replicate the browser environment as much as possible
